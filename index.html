About the Project

Pando Populus is a place-based resilience platform that translates complex environmental and social data into understandable insights. The project supports community awareness, planning, and climate-ready decision-making.

Study Area

The primary study area is Census Tract 1397.02 in Los Angeles County — a coastal community with a stable population, high homeownership, and extensive surrounding open space.

  • Population: ~6,400 residents
  • 24% of residents are age 65+
  • High residential stability
  • Mix of developed land and protected areas

Environmental Risks

Heat

High summer land surface temperatures and limited tree canopy increase heat exposure, particularly for older residents.

Air Quality

PM2.5 pollution exceeds recommended health guidelines, raising respiratory risks during heat and wildfire events.

Flooding

Coastal exposure and watershed connections place portions of the area within current and future flood risk zones.

Wildfire

Surrounding vegetation and regional fire conditions contribute to elevated landscape wildfire hazard.

Resilience Strategies

The project emphasizes multi-benefit, nature-based solutions that address multiple risks simultaneously.

  • Native tree canopy expansion
  • Permeable surfaces and bioswales
  • Shaded and accessible transit corridors
  • Fire-resilient landscaping
  • Climate-ready land use planning

Data & Methods

Pando Populus integrates public datasets including census demographics, environmental indicators, flood and wildfire models, and transit data. Geospatial analysis is used to identify patterns, risks, and opportunities for intervention.

Why It Matters

Climate risks are increasing, but informed planning can reduce harm. This project supports safer communities, healthier ecosystems, and more equitable access to environmental benefits.
